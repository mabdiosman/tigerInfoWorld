Exploring the Tiger's Many Forms

Images: storyteller.travel

Siberian Tiger

The regal monarch of the Siberian taiga, cloaked in a thick, pale coat that blends with the snowy landscapes. These solitary hunters stalk through vast forests, their powerful roars echoing across the frozen tundra.

Bengal Tiger

The undisputed king of the jungle, sporting a fiery orange coat and an imposing presence. Found in India, Nepal, Bangladesh, and Bhutan, their powerful roars echo through lush forests and mangrove swamps.

Indochinese Tiger

The golden ghost of the Southeast Asian jungles, their vibrant orange coat shimmering through the emerald foliage. Expert swimmers, they navigate the Mekong River and its tributaries with unrivaled skill.

Sumatran Tiger

The island king, prowling the dense rainforests of Sumatra. Their distinctive black stripes against a rich orange coat are as unique as their island habitat. These agile climbers scale towering trees with breathtaking grace.
